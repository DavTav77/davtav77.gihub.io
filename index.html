    The Power of Windermills

    Context: Research paper written for CIS-199, Spring 2026. I chose this topic because I wanted to learn more about renewable energy. Windmills specifically caught my interest due to wind being such an abundant resource.

    Wind farms have been around since the 1980s. Ever since then we have seen the potential that Wind Energy brings and have only built upon it. From creating more efficient Wind Turbines, to finding the optimal locations to build this machinery. The power of wind energy has progressed far. Now it is widely recognized for being a cost-effective and in-exhaustible energy source. I will explain why Wind energy is one of the top renewable energy sources that you should keep an eye on.

    It is important understand how Wind Turbines work to recognize why they are so important. With a Wind Turbine, there are large blades facing towards the wind, strong Wind currents are able to spin these blades, which in turn produce kinetic energy. This drives a generator inside of the Turbine, producing the electricity we need. The energy then travels through a series of cable systems, either in the sea floor or on land. If the Turbines are in the ocean, then the electrical channels travel through costal load centers that distribute the electricity into electrical grids that power our everyday life (Hartman 6). With this setup we have been able to use these Wind Turbines to generate a large amount of electricity.

    One of the reasons that Wind Turbines are such a great renewable energy source is because of the very high potential energy output. "technical resource potential for U.S. offshore wind is more than 4,200 gigawatts of capacity, or 13,500 terawatt-hours per year of generation— three times the amount of electricity consumed in the United States annually."(Hartman 1). On off-shore sites Wind Turbines are able to reach their maximum potential. Being able to generate so much electricity allows Wind Turbines to be a reliable source of renewable energy. They also come in a large variety of sizes, allowing us to put them in different places whether it is a wide open land-scape or far into the ocean. "offshore turbines can be scaled up to one-and-a-half times the height of the Washington Monument" (Hartman 2). This indicates to us that Wind Turbines are also very scalable, the limit on these is based on our resources and how to maximize each turbine. Another great addition Wind Turbines bring is convivence to us as a society who relies on electricity for eveyday tasks, "offshore wind speeds are highest during the afternoon and evening, when consumer demand is at its peak"(Hartman 8). Even with all these positives Wind Turbines are not perfect.

    Even though Wind Turbines offer massive potential for renewable energy, it is not consistent across all locations. They can struggle competing with other low-cost energy sources such as Solar Panels in certain locations. "wind projects may not be cost-competitive in some locations that are not windy enough". (Gov 8). This leads to them not always being as reliable and may even be better off being substituted with solar panels in certain areas altogether. Another trade off is that Ideal locations are usually in remote areas. This leads to the process of transferring the energy from a remote location to where it’s needed being a costly process. (Gov 9). Adding on to the concern of price, the resources needed to make the turbines have steadily increased in cost over time. "Steel is one of the core materials used in turbines and plenty of it, but in 2022, the price of steel increased by 86% in the U.S. and 53% in Europe."(Zamir 5). Overtime the prices will continue to rise and make Wid Turbines a huge investment, much more than that what was considered when we have first been investing into them. There is also a technological gap growing in the industry. Wind Turbines have not been able to utilize the latest advancements in technology in the same pace that other renewable sources have."Even those who have adopted new tech are faced with managing different dashboards and systems throughout normal operations." ( Zamir 17). Even when considering all these difficulties, we have still been pushing the limits of what Wind Turbines can bring to the table. The future is still looking bright for these resources

    A key advancement with Wind Turbines that we have always been pushing forward is a focus on maximizing the efficiency of locations where we do know the Wind is working at it’s best. With a focus on creating more efficient Wind Turbines, the limits of what they can power is always being expanded." GE’s Haliade-X gained the distinction of largest turbine model when it went operational, raising the industry benchmark to 13MW." (UTM 3). This focus on maximizing efficiency allows us to increase our energy output without have to make any additional sacrifices. This will "reducing the cost per megawatt and making offshore wind farms more economically viable"(UTM 5 ) Allowing us to avoid having to build Wind Turbines in inefficient locations. We have also improved on installation methods overtime for these over-shore Turbines. The future is moving towards constructing the structures on dry docks. These are platforms that float over water, once finished the structure can dragged on the platforms over to installation sites, reducing the work spent on building Machinery in water by breaking it up into parts on land. Maintenance has also began to become more efficient for the Turbines. Companies have began to use AI and robotics to assist with maintaince on off-shore sites. "Autonomous drones and underwater robots are now being used for inspections and repairs, reducing the need for human intervention in hazardous environments" (UTM 10). Not only will this reduce costs by extending the life of the most valuable Wind Turbines meaning we do not need to create from scratch as often. This will additionally improve efficiency and safety for off-shore workers.

    In conclusion, wind energy has been around for a long time and will continue to be the future for renewable energy. Wind turbines alone have the potential to generate enough energy to power the US, and offer great scalability allowing us to fit them wherever we need them to be. Even though the cost will continue to rise for making these machines, we have also become more efficient on extending the lifetime of the Wind Turbines, while maximizing the use of each machine. This is why I believe Wind energy is one of the pillars of renewable energy and will continue to be in the future.
